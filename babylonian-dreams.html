Dream Interpretation in Babylon

In the civilization of the Babylonians, dreams were believed to be messages sent by the gods. People often asked priests and scholars to interpret their dreams.

Ancient clay tablets discovered in Mesopotamia contain some of the earliest dream interpretation records in human history.

Babylonian dream interpreters believed that some dreams predicted the future, while others warned about dangers or important life decisions.

Common dream symbols included water, stars, animals, and the sky. Each symbol had a meaning connected to divine guidance and destiny.

These interpretations influenced later traditions of dream analysis across many cultures.
